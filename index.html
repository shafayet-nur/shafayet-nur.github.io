Hello, this is Shafayet,

I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Computer Science at Northern Illinois University (NIU), USA, where I am advised by Dr. Hamed Alhoori. I am also a member of the NIU DATALab. My research focuses on applying Large Language Models to scholarly data discovery, with the broader aim of improving how scientific literature is discovered, analyzed and synthesized. My other interests include natural language processing for low-resource languages, particularly cyberbullying detection and hate speech categorization in Bengali text, as well as computer vision and deep learning for agricultural and industrial imaging.

At NIU, I currently serve as a Graduate Teaching Assistant in the Department of Computer Science. Before joining NIU, I earned my B.Sc. in Computer Science and Engineering from Port City International University (PCIU), Bangladesh, in June 2021. I later served in the same department as a Teaching Assistant from September 2021 to September 2022 and as a Lecturer from October 2022 to June 2025. In those roles, I taught undergraduate courses in Artificial Intelligence, Pattern Recognition, Software Engineering, Algorithms, Discrete Mathematics and Structured Programming, while also supervising undergraduate thesis and project works.

Research Interests

  • Large Language Models for Scholarly Data Discovery — benchmarking LLMs on scientific figure understanding, information extraction from scholarly content, improving how scientific literature is discovered, analyzed and synthesized.
  • NLP for Low-Resource Languages — cyberbullying detection, hate speech categorization, and text analytics in Bengali and Romanized Bengali.
  • Computer Vision & Deep Learning — image classification, multi-label plant disease detection, defect detection in industrial settings.
  • Multimodal Learning — fusion of visual and textual features for biomedical and scientific figure classification.
